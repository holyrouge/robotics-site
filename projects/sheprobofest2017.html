ShepRobo Fest 2017

Mech-Warfare

  • Meet Frederick!

Stony Brook Robotics competes in Shepard University's Robofest with Frederick (Fred, for short), a quadruped robot equipped with lidar technology and a fully automated firing mechanism. The challenge simulates combat in an arena and a scaled terrain. The competition puts robots head to head in a gunslinging battle to determine a victor. The competition, Mech Warfare Robot Competition, brings together a knowledge of computer systems and mechanical adaptation to produce a robot suited to navigate the area.

Our Mechanical Team specializes in the design and production of parts including static parts like legs and mounts as well as systems like the self feeding hopper. Our iterative process and research is all done in our lab from the CAD drawings to manufacturing prototype parts.

Each part is carefully designed to be modular and adaptable and follow an iterative process. Starting with the legs, each leg is pointed as to provide a pivot capable of turning easily as a minimal amount of elastic material prevents the legs from sliding. Designs for attachments were adapted from parts of the Robotis System which were used in creating custom 3D parts. Each attachment for the legs and turret were produced by building on a profile of the mounting parts which is used in the leg tips, extenders, and turret positioning system. The Robotis Servo and Arbotix System is a collection of highly efficient servos used in hobby robotics. In addition to the high performance and quick response time of the servos, the servos are exceptional for it wide range of motion and ease of programming, making it an easy choice to program.

The competition is based on airsoft guns, however safety is a concern for both humans and robots alike. Preventing serious damage in close combat, the airsoft pellets had to be stepped down for a more suitable “safe” range for the robot to play safely. Despite using airsoft in its name, an air accelerant used was not used as an air system would provide troublesome stepping down to low pressures. Instead, our airsoft enthusiast members opted to used a mechanical spring operated system that provided an adjustable spring with no need for refilling air canisters.

Our Electrical Team is the powerhouse of the team, providing professionally designed circuits to protect delicate components while still partitioning power enough for all subsystems. Our boards are custom designed and built specifically for our robot. Combining the Robotis Servo System with radio communication modules, our robot is fully equipped to lunge into battle with fast and powerful movements. The Electrical team works closely with our Software Team providing both radio communication and fine tuning the gait.
