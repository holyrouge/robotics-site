Subteams

What Are Subteams?

Stony Brook Robot Design Team is composed of four subteams: Mechanical, Electrical, Software and Science. The subteams work together to develop each of our projects. Each subteam is composed of subsystems that focus on a specific component of the project, such as the arm or the communication. They are focused on providing their members with hands-on learning opportunities in their specific fields.

Mechanical Subteam

The Mechanical Subteam is dedicated to the development of the mechanical components of our projects. Subteam members have the opportunity to gain an indepth understanding of computer aided design (CAD), machining and fabrication. The subsystems of the Mechanical Subteam include the Chassis, the Suspension and the Arm subsystems.

Electrical Subteam

The Electrical Subteam is focused on the development of the electrical components of our projects. Subteam members have the opportunity to learn about PCB design, microcontroller programming and soldering. The subsystems of the Electrical Subteam include the Power, the Communication and the Drivetrain subsystems.

Software Subteam

The Software Subteam is focused on the development of the software components of our projects. Subteam members have the opportunity to learn object-oriented programming, software design and frameworks. The subsytems of the Software Subteam include the Communication, the GPS, the Graphic User Interface (GUI) and the Vision subsystems.

Science Subteam

The Science Subteam is dedicated to the application of scientific research to meet the goals of our projects. Currently, the Subteam is focused on testing methods of data analysis that will be utilized during the University Rover Challenge (URC) to analyze soil samples.
